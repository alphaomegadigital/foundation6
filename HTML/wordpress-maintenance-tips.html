Tips to maintain your Wordpress site


As a professional Wordpress freelancer and agency owner, I often talk to people who maintain their own Wordpress site who ask about best practises to maintain it.

Now if you're not a very technical person, some of this may be out of reach for you, but for those tech savvy readers following along, here are my best tips to maintain your Wordpress website.


In a test environment make sure your sites are up to date

Keeping your sites with the latest version should be your regular maintenance practice that should be undertaken once in a while if an update is available. While undertaking this task should be done in a test environment to avoid the risk of crashing the sites if updates are done on live sites. If you have a specialised Wordpress hosting environment, then you should be able to launch a staging site from your C panel, update your plugins, PHP version and Wordpress software and test it for breaking changes. If it's all good, then you can publish the site. If not, then you can deactivate plugins one by one and check the console for errors you can debug. Keeping your site updated is important to minimise the risk of being hacked as the versions include changes made by developers.


Learning Gutenberg editor (WordPress 5.0)

Using a local WordPress site in Gutenberg editor, you can make changes to your sites in preview mode whereby you do not make changes to your site. This gives you version control, and you can test out text or elements and how they display on the page before you go live with it. Just remember to save it as a draft and not a public page!


Use a Content Delivery Network

If you really want to enhance your site speed, a CDN is a non negotiable. I use Cloudflare, and use all the available optimisations including minifying CSS/JS files, lossless image compression, deferred CSS/JSS delivery and loading critical CSS to enhance site speed. If you're not getting at least a B or A score on your site speed, you are really hurting your SERP due to slow loading times. People will only wait for so long, before they bounce. CDN's like Cloudflare also come with a firewall, so you can replace your security plugin such as Wordfence and enhance your site speed further.


Lower the number of plugins you use

Using a lot of plugins means your site has to run through a large number of JS/CSS scripts and fonts before your page will load and that really shows on your Core Web Vitals score. It also increases the chance of conflicts between plugins. Usually these are CSS conflicts where several styles are using the same CSS classes, but it can also cause breaking changes to your site functions. Only use plugins that have regular updates and offer customer support. If the plugin hasn't been updated in over 6 months that is a red flag.


Responsive on all devices

Your sites should be not only mobile friendly but also fully responsive on all devices. Considering 60% of your web traffic is from a mobile device this is a non negotiable.


Clear Google console errors

If you right click on your page and use the inspect tool in Chrome, you can see the network and any errors that have come up. This is something you really need to debug regularly as broken Javascript is really going to slow down your site, or cause elements to break.


TLS/SSL certificates should be added to all sites

TLS, formerly referred to as SSL is end to end encryption for data being sent over the internet. This means everything from your users IP address to their credit cards are safe whilst browsing your site. Not having SSL updated will greatly reduce your web traffic. Have you ever been to a site without SSL and google warns you that it is not safe to proceed? That is not very good for business, and most won't proceed.
